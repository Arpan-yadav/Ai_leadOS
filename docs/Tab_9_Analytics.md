# Tab 9: Analytics

## 1. Purpose and Role
The Analytics tab is the "Visual Explorer" of the business. Its role is to take all the raw data generated by the platform (leads captured, emails sent, deals won) and turn it into understandable, visual charts. It exists to answer hard business questions, measure team performance, and find areas for improvement.

## 2. Proposed Alternative Naming Conventions
- **Visual Explorer** (Highlights the interactive nature of the page)
- **Performance Metrics** (Professional and exact)
- **Reports** (Traditional, understood by all managers)

## 3. Key Components and Features

### Dataset Selection Panel
- **What it is:** A series of toggle buttons at the top of the page.
- **Components:** Options like "Lead Sources", "Score vs Value", "Stage Conversion", and "Revenue by Channel".
- **Use Case:** A manager decides what they want to investigate. If they want to see if their marketing spend is working, they select "Lead Sources".

### Visualization Type Selector
- **What it is:** Toggles that change how the data is drawn.
- **Components:** Options for Bar Chart, Line Chart, Donut/Pie, Radar/Spider, and Scatter Plot.
- **Use Case:** Different data looks better in different formats. "Score vs Value" is best viewed as a Scatter Plot to find outliers, while "Revenue by Channel" might be best as a Line Chart over time.

### The Chart Canvas
- **What it is:** The large, interactive graph in the center of the screen.
- **Components:** Dynamic SVG charts rendered via Recharts, with tooltips that appear when you hover over data points.
- **Use Case:** The primary visual tool for spotting trends, dips, and spikes in performance.

### AI Data Scientist Section
- **What it is:** A specialized Gemini AI integration sitting below the charts.
- **Components:** A "Generate Insights" button that produces an Executive Summary, Key Insights, and Recommended Actions based on the currently selected dataset.
- **Use Case:** When a user looks at a chart but doesn't know *what it means*, they click this button. The AI reads the chart for them and explains it in plain English.

### Raw Data Table & Advanced Filters
- **What it is:** A spreadsheet-like view at the very bottom of the page.
- **Components:** Displays the exact numbers behind the charts. It includes a search bar, clickable column headers for sorting, and an "Advanced" filter panel to build complex rules (e.g., `Score > 80`).
- **Use Case:** Used when a user needs to verify the exact numbers, find a specific record, or export a highly specific subset of the data for an external report.

## 4. Navigation & Links to Other Pages
- **From Everywhere:** The Analytics tab is the final destination for data. Every action taken in **Leads**, **Pipeline**, **Tasks**, and **Communications** eventually becomes a data point visualized on this page.
